Why Now?
"Paleolithic normative cognition meets 21st-century normative complexity"
A new science is there
  • Humans are normative animals — specialized mechanisms acquire norms from minimal cues
  • Almost all research covers children learning norms of their own community
  • No unified empirical account of normative ignorance yet exists
The world has transformed
  • Migration, globalization, digital life place us daily in unfamiliar normative environments
  • Legal complexity grows faster than anyone can track
  • Platform norms — algorithmically enforced, opaque, fast-changing
  • Normative ignorance is no longer the exception — it is the rule
People are routinely held responsible under assumptions of knowledge that no longer hold.
Why Me?
Who I am
Trained in law, philosophy, and law & economics — but experiments are what I actually do. After years as a researcher on ERC Biouncertainty and a postdoc at Max Planck (Bonn), I now lead my own NCN project on ignorance of law, working alongside and supervising cognitive scientists and experimental economists.
Why this project
I currently run two interdisciplinary teams — one with cognitive scientists, one with a linguist co-PI. NormaeNescio demands exactly that kind of coordination across methods and disciplines. This is what I have been building toward.
